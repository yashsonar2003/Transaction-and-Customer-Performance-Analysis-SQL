# Objective:
The Goal of this analysis is to understand Revenue Patterns, Customer Behavior, City Performance, and Business Risks using SQL.

-----

# Revenue Insights:
- Completed transactions are the main revenue source.
- Revenue is not evenly distributed across customers.
- A small number of customers contribute a large portion of total revenue.

-----

# Customer Insights:
- Top customers generate significantly higher revenue than average customers.
- Repeat customers are critical for business growth.
- Some customers have not made recent transactions and are considered 'Inactive'.
- Customers with frequent cancellations may impact revenue 'negatively'.

-----

# City-Level Insights:
- Certain cities contribute the 'highest revenue'.
- Some cities show 'higher cancellation rates' compared to others.
- Cities with 'high revenue' and 'low cancellation rates' are the most profitable.

-----

# Risks Identified
- 'High cancellation' rates in specific cities.
- Customers with repeated failed or cancelled transactions.
- 'Inactive customers' reducing future revenue potential.

-----

# Business Opportunities: 
- Re-engagement campaigns for 'inactive customers'.
- Loyalty programs for 'high-value customers'.
- Operational improvements in 'high-cancellation cities'.

-----

# Conclusion:
This SQL analysis helps the business make data-driven decisions by identifying key Revenue Drivers, Customer Behavior Patterns, and Risk Areas.
